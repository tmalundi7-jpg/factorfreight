*Last Updated and Fact-Checked: July 2026*

**The main difference is risk: recourse factoring is cheaper but requires you to buy back unpaid invoices, while non-recourse is more expensive but protects you if the broker goes bankrupt. Both methods still hold you liable for unpaid invoices resulting from freight disputes or damage.**

## Overview
Choosing between recourse and non-recourse factoring is one of the most critical financial decisions for a new carrier. Navigating the recourse vs non recourse factoring pros and cons requires analyzing your risk tolerance, profit margins, and the quality of the brokers you haul for.

## Key Factors to Consider
- **Cost:** Recourse rates typically range from 1% to 3%, while non-recourse rates range from 2.5% to 5%.
- **Risk Assumption:** Recourse places all the credit risk on the carrier. Non-recourse shifts the broker's bankruptcy risk to the factor.
- **Approval Rates:** Recourse factors will often approve a wider variety of brokers since they have the carrier's guarantee as a back-up.

*Practitioner Note:* "In my experience reviewing hundreds of factoring agreements, carriers often overpay for non-recourse factoring when they only haul for mega-brokers with perfect credit. You have to match the product to your actual risk."

## Step-by-Step Process
1. **Assess Your Margins:** If you run incredibly tight margins, the extra 1-2% for non-recourse might wipe out your profit.
2. **Evaluate Your Brokers:** Are you running the spot market with unknown brokers, or dedicated lanes with CH Robinson?
3. **Read the Contract:** Understand [what happens if broker doesnt pay non recourse factoring](/recourse-vs-non-recourse/what-happens-if-broker-doesnt-pay-non-recourse-factoring) to see the limits of the protection.
4. **Make a Selection:** Choose the path that lets you sleep at night without destroying your bottom line. Read our [non recourse freight factoring guide](/recourse-vs-non-recourse/non-recourse-freight-factoring-guide) for more details.

## Common Mistakes & Pitfalls
- **Misunderstanding Non-Recourse:** Thinking [is non recourse factoring really risk free](/recourse-vs-non-recourse/is-non-recourse-factoring-really-risk-free)? It is not. You are always liable for freight disputes.
- **Focusing Only on Rate:** Choosing recourse just because it's cheaper, then being bankrupted by a single $5,000 broker default.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)
**Can I switch from recourse to non-recourse later?**
Yes, most factoring companies will allow you to upgrade your contract or switch to non-recourse at renewal time.
