**Recourse factoring personal guarantee requirements mandate that the owner of the trucking company is personally liable for any unpaid invoices or contract fees. If your LLC fails and cannot buy back a defaulted invoice, the factoring company can sue you personally and place liens on your personal assets.**

## Overview
Many owner-operators form an LLC to separate their personal assets from their business liabilities. However, the recourse factoring personal guarantee requirements completely pierce that corporate veil. If you sign a personal guarantee, your house, personal bank accounts, and vehicles are on the line if a broker defaults and your business can't cover the chargeback.

## Key Factors to Consider
- **The Ultimate Safety Net:** Factors require personal guarantees because trucking LLCs often have zero tangible assets (the truck is usually financed by a bank). You are the only source of collateral.
- **Validity Clause:** A personal guarantee legally binds you as an individual. It supersedes the protections of your LLC or Corporation.
- **Spousal Signatures:** In some states, factors may require your spouse to sign the guarantee to ensure they can access jointly held property in the event of a default.

*Practitioner Note:* "In my experience reviewing hundreds of factoring agreements, it is nearly impossible for an owner-operator with a new authority to secure factoring without signing a personal guarantee, regardless of whether it is recourse or non-recourse. The factor needs leverage."

## Step-by-Step Process (Understanding the Guarantee)
1. **Identify the Clause:** In your Master Factoring Agreement, look for the section titled "Validity Guarantee" or "Personal Guaranty."
2. **Understand the Scope:** Does the guarantee cover just the unpaid invoices, or does it also cover legal fees, early termination penalties, and minimum volume fees?
3. **Assess the Risk:** In a recourse contract, the risk is much higher because you are guaranteeing *every* invoice. Read [recourse factoring what happens if broker goes bankrupt](/recourse-vs-non-recourse/recourse-factoring-what-happens-if-broker-goes-bankrupt).
4. **Consider Non-Recourse:** While non-recourse still requires a personal guarantee, the factor assumes the credit risk, meaning you are less likely to face a catastrophic personal lawsuit over a broker's bankruptcy. Compare options in [recourse vs non recourse factoring pros and cons](/recourse-vs-non-recourse/recourse-vs-non-recourse-factoring-pros-and-cons).

## Common Mistakes & Pitfalls
- **Assuming the LLC Protects You:** Believing that simply having an LLC means the factor can't touch your personal savings. 
- **Ignoring the Clause:** Skimming the contract and not realizing you signed away your personal asset protection.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)
**Can I negotiate the personal guarantee out of the contract?**
If you are a single-truck owner-operator or a new authority, no. Only large fleets with millions in hard assets and verified financial statements can sometimes negotiate the removal of a personal guarantee.
