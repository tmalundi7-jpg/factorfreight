# Security Standards for Factoring Portals

**Security standards for factoring portals require bank-level 256-bit AES encryption, Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA), role-based access controls, and strict compliance with data privacy regulations to protect your sensitive financial information.**

## Overview
Factoring portals hold the keys to your cash flow. If a hacker gains access, they can misdirect thousands of dollars in wire transfers. Understanding the security standards for factoring portals is non-negotiable when evaluating a new financial partner. Security should be the first feature you look for when exploring [factoring company portals what features matter](/software-and-integrations/factoring-company-portals-what-features-matter).

## Key Factors to Consider
- **Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA):** This is the single most important feature. Every login must require a code sent to your phone or an authenticator app.
- **Session Timeouts:** The portal must automatically log you out after 10-15 minutes of inactivity to prevent unauthorized access if you leave your laptop open.
- **Audit Logs:** The portal should track the IP address and timestamp of every login and every requested wire transfer.

## Step-by-Step Process
1. **Ask for the Security Policy:** Before signing a contract, ask the factor, "Is your portal SOC 2 compliant?"
2. **Enable MFA:** The moment you create your account, navigate to settings and turn on Multi-Factor Authentication.
3. **Restrict Access:** Use role-based permissions. Only the business owner should have the ability to change the destination bank account for wire transfers. See [factoring software for managing multiple trucks](/software-and-integrations/factoring-software-for-managing-multiple-trucks).
4. **Monitor Audit Logs:** Check your login history monthly to ensure there are no unrecognizable IP addresses accessing your account.

## Common Mistakes & Pitfalls
- **Shared Passwords:** Giving your master login credentials to your dispatcher. If they leave the company on bad terms, your finances are compromised.
- **Ignoring Software Updates:** If you use a mobile app, failing to install updates leaves you vulnerable to known security exploits. Read about [mobile apps for freight factoring owner operators](/software-and-integrations/mobile-apps-for-freight-factoring-owner-operators).

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)
**What happens if my factoring portal gets hacked?**
If the breach is on the factor's end, they are generally liable for replacing stolen funds. If the breach was because you gave out your password or fell for a phishing scam, you might be held responsible.
**How do I know if an API integration is secure?**
Ensure the API uses OAuth 2.0 authentication and never hardcode API keys. Learn more in [API access for freight factoring companies](/software-and-integrations/api-access-for-freight-factoring-companies).
