# Automated Reconciliation Tools for Factoring

**Automated reconciliation tools for factoring match incoming bank deposits to open invoices and automatically split out the factoring fee and reserve holdbacks, turning hours of manual accounting into a single-click process.**

## Overview
Reconciling a factoring statement manually involves calculating the advance rate, subtracting the fee, tracking the reserve, and matching the final wire to the bank account. Automated reconciliation tools for factoring solve this massive headache, seamlessly connecting your portal to your ledger. This is a critical feature when evaluating the [best factoring software integrations for trucking](/software-and-integrations/best-factoring-software-integrations-for-trucking).

## Key Factors to Consider
*Practitioner Note:* "In my experience, carriers who do not use automated reconciliation eventually give up tracking their reserves entirely. They just treat the wire transfer as income, which leads to massive tax overpayments and total blindness to how much the factoring company actually owes them."
- **Direct Accounting Sync:** The tool must map data directly to standard accounts (A/R, Fees, Reserves). See [how to track factoring payments in accounting software](/software-and-integrations/how-to-track-factoring-payments-in-accounting-software).
- **Chargeback Handling:** The tool must be smart enough to recognize a chargeback and automatically adjust your accounts receivable ledger.
- **Batch Processing:** It should handle a wire transfer that covers 15 different invoices in one bulk matching action.

## Step-by-Step Process
1. **Connect Your Ledger:** Link your factoring portal to QuickBooks Online, Xero, or your TMS. Read [how to integrate QuickBooks with freight factoring](/software-and-integrations/how-to-integrate-quickbooks-with-freight-factoring).
2. **Map the Accounts:** Tell the software which general ledger account represents "Factoring Fees" and which represents "Factoring Reserve."
3. **Fetch Data:** Trigger the software to pull the settlement report from the factoring company.
4. **Review the Match:** The software will suggest matches (e.g., "Wire of $9,500 matches Invoices A, B, and C").
5. **Approve and Reconcile:** Click "Approve," and the software makes the journal entries automatically.

## Common Mistakes & Pitfalls
- **Incorrect Mapping:** If you map the factoring fee to an "Asset" account instead of an "Expense" account, your balance sheet will be completely wrong.
- **Ignoring the Dashboard Alerts:** If the automated tool flags a discrepancy (like a short payment), you must investigate it immediately. Use your [custom dashboard for freight factoring data](/software-and-integrations/custom-dashboard-for-freight-factoring-data) to track these.
