# How to Connect DAT Load Board With Factoring

**To connect the DAT load board with your factoring company, you typically use a Chrome extension, API key integration, or TMS middleware to automatically display broker credit approval icons directly next to posted loads.**

## Overview
Knowing how to connect DAT load board with factoring transforms your load booking process. Instead of toggling between the DAT interface and your factoring portal to check MC numbers, the integration overlays approval statuses directly onto the load board. This is a premier example of the [best factoring software integrations for trucking](/software-and-integrations/best-factoring-software-integrations-for-trucking).

## Key Factors to Consider
*Practitioner Note:* "In my experience reviewing hundreds of factoring agreements, carriers who integrate their load boards with their factoring software reduce their deadhead miles and booking times by up to 30%, simply because they can instantly filter out bad-credit brokers."

- **Browser Extensions:** Many factoring companies provide a Google Chrome extension for DAT Power.
- **API Availability:** Check if your factoring company offers an API for deeper TMS integrations. [Read about API access for freight factoring companies](/software-and-integrations/api-access-for-freight-factoring-companies).
- **Subscription Levels:** Ensure your DAT subscription level supports third-party integrations.

## Step-by-Step Process
1. **Download the Extension:** Navigate to the Chrome Web Store and download your factoring company's official extension.
2. **Log In:** Authenticate the extension using your factoring portal credentials.
3. **Open DAT:** Log into your DAT account in the same browser.
4. **Verify the Overlay:** You should now see green checkmarks, yellow warnings, or red X's next to broker names indicating their credit status. [Learn about automated broker credit checks factoring software](/software-and-integrations/automated-broker-credit-checks-factoring-software).

## Common Mistakes & Pitfalls
- **Outdated Software:** Failing to update the browser extension can lead to inaccurate credit data displaying on the load board.
- **Assuming Global Approval:** A green checkmark usually means approved *up to a certain limit*. Always verify the specific dollar amount for high-value loads.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Does this integration cost extra?**
Most large factoring companies offer their load board browser extensions for free as a value-add for their clients.

**Can I connect other load boards besides DAT?**
Yes, many of these extensions and integrations also work with Truckstop.com and other major platforms. For more info on broker standards, refer to the [FMCSA Guidelines](https://www.fmcsa.dot.gov/).
